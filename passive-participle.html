Passive Participle (اسم المفعول)

Introduction to the Passive Participle

The Passive Participle in Arabic (اسم المفعول) is a noun derived from a verb that indicates the receiver of an action. It describes someone or something that has been acted upon or affected by an action.

Similar to the Active Participle, it can also function as an adjective in English, providing additional information about the subject.

Formation of the Passive Participle

The Passive Participle is generally formed from the root of a verb, and its formation can vary based on the verb form. Here are the common patterns for different verb forms:

Form I (فعل ثلاثي مجرد)

For Form I verbs, the Passive Participle is typically formed by adding a ماء (م) prefix to the root letters.

Examples:

  • مكتوب (maktub) - written (from كتب - kataba, meaning "to write").
  • مدروس (madroos) - studied (from درس - darasa, meaning "to study").

Form II (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

For Form II verbs, the Passive Participle is formed by adding a ماء (م) prefix and doubling the second root letter.

Examples:

  • مكسّر (muksar) - broken (from كسّر - kassara, meaning "to break").
  • معلّم (mu'allim) - taught (from درّس - darrasa, meaning "to teach").

Form III (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

For Form III verbs, the Passive Participle is formed by adding a ماء (م) prefix.

Examples:

  • مشارك (musharak) - participated (from شارك - sharika, meaning "to participate").
  • مقاتل (muqatil) - fought (from قاتل - qatal, meaning "to fight").

Form IV (فعل رباعي)

For Form IV verbs, the Passive Participle is formed similarly, adding a ماء (م) prefix before the first root letter.

Examples:

  • مكتب (maktab) - made to write (from أكتب - aktaba, meaning "to make (someone) write").
  • مسمع (masma'a) - made to listen (from أسمع - asma'a, meaning "to make (someone) listen").

Form V (فعل خماسي)

For Form V verbs, the Passive Participle is formed similarly by adding a ماء (م) prefix.

Examples:

  • متعلّم (muta'allim) - learned (from تعلّم - ta'allama, meaning "to learn").
  • متكلّم (mutakallim) - spoken (from تكلم - takallama, meaning "to speak").

Uses of the Passive Participle

The Passive Participle can be used in various contexts:

  • To describe something that has been done: الكتاب مكتوب. (The book is written.)
  • To express a characteristic: الدرس مدروس جيدًا. (The lesson is well-studied.)
  • As a noun to refer to the receiver of the action: المشارك في البرنامج مدعوم. (The participant in the program is supported.)
