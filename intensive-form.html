Intensive Form (المبالغة)

Introduction to the Intensive Form

The Intensive Form in Arabic (المبالغة) is used to express a high degree of an action or a quality. It often indicates that someone is an expert or exhibits a particular characteristic to an extreme level.

This form is typically derived from the root of a verb and follows specific patterns, mainly based on the verb's form.

Formation of the Intensive Form

The Intensive Form is primarily formed from Form I verbs, and it can be constructed by following these common patterns:

Form I (فعل ثلاثي مجرد)

For Form I verbs, the Intensive Form is usually formed by repeating the second root letter and adding a vowel at the beginning.

Examples:

  • كتّاب (kattab) - writer (from كتب - kataba, meaning "to write").
  • مُدرّس (mudarris) - teacher (from درس - darasa, meaning "to study").

Form II (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

The Intensive Form can also be derived from Form II verbs, typically by altering the structure slightly.

Examples:

  • مُعَلّم (mu'allim) - teacher (from درّس - darrasa, meaning "to teach").
  • مُصمّم (musammim) - designer (from صمّم - sammama, meaning "to design").

Form III (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

The Intensive Form for Form III verbs may follow a similar pattern as well.

Examples:

  • مُشارك (musharik) - participant (from شارك - sharika, meaning "to participate").
  • مُستشار (mustashar) - consultant (from استشار - ishtashara, meaning "to consult").

Uses of the Intensive Form

The Intensive Form is used in various contexts, such as:

  • To emphasize a particular quality: الكاتب كتّاب موهوب. (The writer is a talented author.)
  • To indicate someone who performs an action intensively: المعلّم مُدرّس متميز. (The teacher is an outstanding instructor.)
  • To express a high degree of the action: اللاعب مُهاري. (The player is skilled.)
