Active Participle (اسم الفاعل)

Introduction to the Active Participle

The Active Participle in Arabic (اسم الفاعل) is a noun derived from a verb that indicates the doer of an action. It functions similarly to an adjective in English and provides additional information about the subject of a sentence.

Active participles can be used to describe someone who is currently performing an action or someone characterized by that action.

Formation of the Active Participle

The Active Participle is formed from the root of a verb, usually following specific patterns based on the verb form. Here are the common patterns for different verb forms:

Form I (فعل ثلاثي مجرد)

For Form I verbs, the Active Participle is typically formed by adding a باء (ب) prefix to the root letters, followed by the appropriate vowel endings.

Examples:

  • كاتب (katib) - writer (from كتب - kataba, meaning "to write").
  • دارس (daras) - student (from درس - darasa, meaning "to study").

Form II (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

For Form II verbs, the Active Participle is formed by doubling the second root letter and adding a باء (ب) prefix.

Examples:

  • معلّم (mu'allim) - teacher (from درّس - darrasa, meaning "to teach").
  • مكسّر (mukassir) - breaker (from كسّر - kassara, meaning "to break").

Form III (فعل ثلاثي مزيد بحرف)

For Form III verbs, the Active Participle is formed by adding a باء (ب) prefix and following the same pattern.

Examples:

  • مشارك (musharik) - participant (from شارك - sharika, meaning "to participate").
  • مقاتل (muqatil) - fighter (from قاتل - qatal, meaning "to fight").

Form IV (فعل رباعي)

For Form IV verbs, the Active Participle is formed by adding a باء (ب) prefix before the first root letter.

Examples:

  • مكتب (maktab) - one who makes someone write (from أكتب - aktaba, meaning "to make (someone) write").
  • مسمع (masma'a) - one who makes someone listen (from أسمع - asma'a, meaning "to make (someone) listen").

Form V (فعل خماسي)

For Form V verbs, the Active Participle is formed similarly by adding a باء (ب) prefix.

Examples:

  • متعلّم (muta'allim) - learner (from تعلّم - ta'allama, meaning "to learn").
  • متكلّم (mutakallim) - speaker (from تكلم - takallama, meaning "to speak").

Uses of the Active Participle

The Active Participle can be used in several ways:

  • To describe someone performing an action: الكاتب يكتب كتابًا. (The writer is writing a book.)
  • To express a characteristic: المعلم صبور. (The teacher is patient.)
  • As a noun to refer to the doer: المشارك في المسابقة فاز. (The participant in the competition won.)
